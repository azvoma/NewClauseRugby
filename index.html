FAQ

Common Questions

How much does commercial EV charger installation cost? +
Commercial EV charger installation typically costs between £800–£3,500 per unit depending on charger type, site requirements and cabling work. Businesses can claim up to 75% back via the Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS), capped at £350 per socket with a maximum of 40 sockets. Your installer will guide you through the application process.
What is the Workplace Charging Scheme? +
The Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS) is a UK government grant providing up to 75% off the cost of purchasing and installing EV charge points. It's available to eligible businesses, charities and public sector organisations and is administered through OZEV (the Office for Zero Emission Vehicles). All installers in our directory are OZEV-approved and can help you apply.
Do I need an OZEV-approved installer? +
Yes — to qualify for government grants such as the Workplace Charging Scheme, you must use an OZEV-approved installer. All companies listed in this directory hold current OZEV approval, along with Part P certification and relevant electrical qualifications.
How long does a commercial EV charger installation take? +
Most commercial EV charger installations take 1–3 days for a small number of units. Larger installations involving groundworks, DNO applications or multiple charge points may take 1–2 weeks. Your installer will conduct a site survey to provide an accurate timeline.
What types of businesses can get commercial EV chargers? +
Any business, charity or public sector organisation can install commercial EV chargers. Common applications include offices, warehouses, retail sites, car parks, hotels, schools, NHS facilities, fleet depots and multi-unit residential developments. Our directory includes specialists for every sector.
